The Quest — Daily Practice
PRESS
Making the Soul Happy in God — The First Work of Every Day
P
Pour Out
Bring what is actually there, not what you think should be there.
⌄
“Pour out your heart before Him; God is a refuge for us.”
— Psalm 62:8 (LSB)
Before anything else, bring what is actually in you. The cold heart. The distracted mind. The unfinished worries. He already knows. Coming honestly is the first act of trust.
R
Remember
Before I bring my needs, I bring my worship.
⌄
“Bless the LORD, O my soul, and all that is within me, bless His holy name. Forget none of His benefits.”
— Psalm 103:1–2 (LSB)
A Psalm. A hymn. One truth about who He is, held before everything else. Worship is not the warm-up. It is the orientation that makes everything after it right.
E
Eat
Read to find Him, not to finish.
⌄
“Your words were found and I ate them, and Your words became for me a joy and the delight of my heart.”
— Jeremiah 15:16 (LSB)
Stay where something moves. Ask why it moves you. Not information, but encounter. It could come as increased clarity, stronger desire, depth of peace, Truth affecting your heart, sin convicting, internal steadiness, real gladness, great joy. The response is His to give.
S
Speak
Let what I just read become what I say back to Him.
⌄
“In everything by prayer and petition with thanksgiving let your requests be made known to God.”
— Philippians 4:6 (LSB)
Respond to what He just said. Worship what His character reveals. Confess what it exposes. Ask for what it promises. The Word and prayer are one conversation.
S
Stay
Don’t leave when I have technically done something.
⌄
“Wait for the LORD; be strong and let your heart take courage; yes, wait for the LORD.”
— Psalm 27:14 (LSB)
Stay until the heart is actually glad in Him. Go back through P–R–E–S again if needed, each time with a warmer heart and more capacity for deeper connection. This is the hardest step and the most important one.
The goal is not completion. It is gladness in Him.
Did I leave with my soul more glad in God than when I arrived?
I press in until I can press in, and then I press in.
